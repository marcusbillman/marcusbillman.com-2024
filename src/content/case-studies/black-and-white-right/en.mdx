# Introduction

More and more modern user interfaces feature a dark theme with dark text on a light background, opposite of the traditional approach. Many people find this more comfortable to use and it also brings practical benefits such as lower power consumption on OLED displays. Among my generation, it is even common for dark theme users to actively dislike light themes. Being in upper secondary school at the time, this got me thinking—can I measure a difference in usability due to the colour theme among my fellow students?

# Methods

For this study, I created a test website with a light and dark variant. Half of my participants were shown the light version and the other half saw the dark one. The participants answered a survey about their opinion and experience of the site and its usability. Some also participated in user tests with tasks such as reading, finding links and filling forms. This way, I hoped to compare the two colour themes in a measurable way.

# Results

The survey found that the dark website was perceived as slightly more appealing among the participants and the majority generally preferred dark apps and websites, which may be related to the fact that most were in dark rooms when answering. The website's light and dark variants were both perceived as clearly structured, comfortable to read and free of distractions, but dark theme had the edge in certain aspects.

Meanwhile in the interviews, the light theme users read on average 21% faster and performed better when recalling what they'd read. However, reading speed varied significantly between users of the same color theme, suggesting that reading habits have a larger impact than the color theme. The remaining tasks, such as finding links or filling a contact form, were done equally well on both themes.

# Conclusion

Participants of the survey did lean towards the dark theme, but not enough to crown it a clear winner. A website's readability and usability are significantly more affected by the user's literacy, habits and environment than by the color theme, assuming the website follows basic usability practices. This may be a disappointing conclusion, but because people have different preferences, the winner is the ability to choose.
